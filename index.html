About

Social Hacks is a 24-hour, student-run hackathon that brings together students, programmers, technologists, and activists in New York City to collaborate on technological solutions to the toughest challenges that humanity faces today—poverty, climate change, global health, cybersecurity, and more!

Themes

We'll do prizes on these four topics, but feel free to pursue your own!

Globalization

Increased global trade has spurred economic growth, poverty reduction, and cross-cultural understanding, but it has also damaged the environment and destabilized local communities. How can we use technology to help people and communities deal with the negative effects of globalization?

Mental Health

Nearly one in five Americans struggle with mental health problems, and mental illness is more widespread than most people think. Anxiety disorders, depression, and drug addiction are among the most common mental health issues in the United States. How can we use technology to help people deal with mental health issues?

Inequality

This category includes economic inequality as well as social inequalities such as institutional racism, sexism, homophobia, transphobia, and classism. In America and around the world, women, people of color, and the poor often have limited opportunities because of systemic discrimination and lack of access. How can we use technology to help create a more fair, more equal society?

Climate Change

Climate change due to industrialization and heavy use of fossil fuels claims over 300,000 lives and costs the world economy $125 billion USD per year, and developing countries feel most of the heat because of worsening floods and droughts. How can we use technology to reduce our carbon footprint and protect the environment?

FAQ


What is Social Hacks?

Social Hacks is a hackathon where hackers design hardware and software to solve a social issue or help others better understand an issue.

What counts as a social issue?

We broadly define a “social issue” as any problem that affects a community—including economic, civil liberties/civil rights, health, and environmental issues. Social issues overlap heavily with political issues, but we’d like our hackers to think beyond the issues that politicians talk about all the time.

Who should attend?

Anyone who is a high school or college student is welcome to attend! Whether you are a beginner or intermediate hacker, people of any level of skill set can attend.


What do I bring?

Besides your laptop and any necessary technology, you should also bring any toiletries and sleeping bag for the overnight stay! Any food and drinks will be provided by us. :)

How do I form or join a team?

We encourage participants to come with a team beforehand but if you don’t have a team, that’s alright! There’ll be a team networking event where you can meet people like you who are ready to build the next big thing!

So, what can hackers make?

Broadly speaking, any technological solution or partial solution to a social issue or software project that helps us better understand an issue (such as a data analysis program, a website, or a computer animation) that involves a significant amount of engineering and/or computer programming.

What's the Code of Conduct?

All hackers, mentors, sponsors, and organizers who participate in Social Hacks must follow the Hack Code of Conduct throughout the event. Basically, this means no harassment or discrimination of any kind, including bullying, inappropriate touching, or photographing anyone who has explicitly asked you not to.


If you have any other questions not listed above, feel free to shoot us an email at socialhacksnyc@gmail.com!
